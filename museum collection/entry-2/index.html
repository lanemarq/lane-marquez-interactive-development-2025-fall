Dyeing My Hair

Blue

1.

In a way I (wonder which color I should get),
Hope that (I won’t regret this) I can find peace in my breathing impulsivity,
Carried by the subtlety of my (foolish), trembling self, and the reflection that looks at me (Hi there!),
I can be more than what people say I am (a girl).

The cheapest dye and bleach (how dumb of me, whoops) that my sprouting soul could buy,
With money (I barely even had, mind you), carrying promises and expectations from,
Who knows where (I knew, really), but I can learn the map from the way of the paper,
Filled with lines of how to not burn my scalp and hair off (and how not to piss off my parents).

When the bleach falls on my hair for the first time,
And the blue takes away my blonde, with the shower changing along with my hair
The blue stains never fully came out, now being, the evidence of,
My presence in these odd, high school, dorms.

The ends, left dead and brittle through cheap bleach and dye,
I swore to myself to never again do it, it's too damaging, too much for my hair,
I’ll never do it again, it just washed out too quickly, too oddly,
Maybe it just wasn’t for me.

Red

2.

And when I looked at myself in the mirror and saw the evidence of my reflection,
The longest part of my fresh, alive, wolf cut, that bright, vivid, alive red
I was nothing more than the feeling of an external force,
Of waving euphoria in my throat reaching the corners of my smiling mouth.

(Turns out my real mistake was trusting splat bleach and hair dye that first time, haha.)
(I think the red in my hair fits me way better than the blue ever did, don’t you think?)
(Sally’s has this colored conditioner I could use to keep it vibrant. I should go there soon.)
(The only con is that it looks like a bloodbath whenever I wash my hair. Yiiiiikes.)

It feels like, I just discovered the simple act of finding something more,
About the inner workings of the crevices engulfing my mind and body,
Finding more than, what I thought I only had,
It softens, the weight of that fragment.

(The conditioner for my hair was ten whole dollars. In THIS economy?)
(Everytime it rains, I have to pray to whoever will listen to me so that my clothes don’t turn red.)
(But it's soooo worth it though. Can you believe I just didn’t dye my hair for that long?)
(Though my friend called and told me they found a red strand of hair in their house, how did that happen?)

It was the invitation my fingers felt that allowed me to figure this out more,
I can’t always stay the same after all, I morph and shape and grow older with time and water,
Oddly shaped brows and chokers, glasses I couldn’t use.
I feel that, I might slowly be figuring it out.

Orange

3.

(I went with orange so that it would be that color by Halloween.)
(And also because I didn’t want to just stop at red, that would be silly, I think.)
(There’s colors I haven’t tried. I want to try them all.)
(Other than like. Maybe yellow. I don’t know. Yellow is okay.)

(The orange isn’t as vibrant as I would like, I wish I waited to bleach my hair more.)
(But black hair is so hard to bleach, it feels like I have to burn my hair off just to get it the way I want.)
(And then it feels so. Dry. Like the texture of hair those dolls had that my mama always tried to get me to play with.)
(Maybe I should get it bleached professionally, but that’s crazy expensive. I need to buy groceries this week.)

Strands I can’t afford to lose,
To the ones, and twos, of colored water and the salt from my body,
I realize that, I could take control of it,
And the light flickers more.

Purple

4.

(My best friend cut my hair and dyed it whenever I was over at their parents’ house…)

I always carry, the same things you gave in,
Scattered polaroids and breathing through teeth,
With the tattoo on my, left thigh,
I’ll carry you with me, too.

(If you’re reading this, I miss you bestie. Let’s go get Taco Bell whenever you’re home.)

Green

5.

(This is my current hair color, I really like it a lot, though I really should redye it before Friday.)

All of my pulsing, gripped droplets of sweat and countless repressed cries I wanted so badly to,

(I’m so tired though.)

Let out like the sound of my brake pads that I cannot afford to replace,

(I would rather go to bed than spend time redyeing my hair.)

I’ll see, the efforts of my work in a vivid vision I glance at through my faded, swollen corners,

(Maybe, life isn’t all about working. Maybe it's just about living and enjoying it. Isn’t that crazy?)

And feel that same flicker of the match finally, set me free.
